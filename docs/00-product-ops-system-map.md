# Product Ops System Map

Product Operations helps teams turn product signals into better operating decisions.

This sandbox models the loop:

```text
feedback + research + product data
-> insights
-> opportunities
-> prioritization
-> roadmap candidates
-> OKR alignment
-> release communication and measurement
-> learning for the next planning cycle
```

## Why The Loop Matters

Product teams often have useful signals in different places:

| Signal | Usually Comes From | Product Ops Question |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Product analytics | Event tracking and dashboards | What is happening? |
| Customer feedback | Support, sales, success, reviews | What are users saying? |
| User research | Interviews and studies | Why might this be happening? |
| Roadmap data | PM planning and strategy | What should we consider next? |
| OKRs | Company and product planning | What outcome should improve? |
| Release candidates | Product, engineering, and GTM planning | How should teams prepare for launch? |

The value is not one perfect answer. The value is a repeatable way to connect evidence, judgment, and follow up measurement.

## Operating Principle

```text
Code calculates.
AI drafts.
Humans decide.
```

Deterministic scripts are useful when the input is structured and the logic should be repeatable. AI assisted workflows are useful when the input is qualitative and needs synthesis. Human review is required in both lanes.

## Planning Chain

Use this chain to understand how the later outputs connect:

```text
Signals explain what is happening.
Insights explain why it may matter.
Opportunities define what could improve.
Prioritization compares what deserves attention.
Roadmap candidates show what the team may build.
OKR alignment connects work to measurable outcomes.
Release planning prepares teams for launch.
Measurement checks whether the change worked.
```

OKRs are an alignment layer. They should not be generated only from raw feedback. Product teams use strategy, company priorities, and current product outcomes to define OKRs. Product Ops then helps connect evidence, roadmap candidates, release plans, and measurement back to those outcomes.
