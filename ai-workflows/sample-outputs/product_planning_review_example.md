# Product Planning Review

This is a fictional mock AI output. It shows how signals can connect to planning decisions, but it is still a draft for human review.

## Evidence Chain

| Layer | What It Shows |
| --- | --- |
| Signal | Support notes and metrics show failed check ins and support contacts around gym visits. |
| Insight | Users lose confidence when the app says they can use the benefit but the partner visit does not work smoothly. |
| Opportunity | Improve check in recovery so users and partners know what to do when the first scan fails. |
| Prioritization | The roadmap score ranks check in fallback highest because it combines reach, impact, confidence, effort, and strategic fit. |
| Roadmap candidate | Improve check in fallback. |
| OKR connection | This work may support reliable first value and reduce failed check ins. |
| Release planning | Support and partner facing teams need a clear message before launch. |
| Measurement | Track failed check ins and support contacts after release. |

## Planning Assessment

The strongest planning thread is check in reliability. It appears in metrics, feedback themes, opportunity mapping, and roadmap scoring.

The evidence is directional because the dataset is small and fictional. A real team should confirm whether the pattern is concentrated by partner, city, device, or employer launch.

## Roadmap Review

| Candidate | Why It Deserves Discussion | Review Needed |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Improve check in fallback | Strongest link between feedback, metric, and roadmap score. | Confirm partner concentration and support impact. |
| Clarify booking confirmation | High severity feedback and clear user confidence problem. | Confirm how often users arrive without confidence. |
| Launch activation reminder kit | Connected to employer value story and activation. | Confirm whether activation issues are product, communication, or eligibility related. |

## OKR Connection

The likely OKR connection is:

```text
Objective: Help employees reach reliable first value in the wellness journey.
Key Result: Reduce failed check ins.
```

This should be treated as alignment, not approval. A product leader should decide whether this is a current quarter objective, a future objective, or simply a supporting metric.

## Decision Risks

- A high roadmap score does not prove the solution is correct.
- Support notes may overrepresent users who had a bad experience.
- Failed check ins may come from partner operations, device setup, app logic, or user confusion.
- The team should not launch without a measurement plan.

## Next Review

Review failed check ins, support contacts, first successful visits, and partner concentration after the next release candidate is tested.
